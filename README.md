# Profit Per Job Calculator

## Overview
The **Profit Per Job Calculator** is a premium, automated Excel-based tool specifically designed for owner-operators in the residential exterior cleaning industry (window, gutter, siding, soft washing, and pressure washing). This calculator transforms pricing from guesswork into data-driven certainty, helping businesses eliminate unprofitable jobs and maximize their bottom line.

Built by an exterior cleaner, for exterior cleaners, this tool addresses core pain points such as pricing by guesswork, hidden cost bleed, and the devastating impact of callbacks. It offers a one-time investment solution that stands apart from generic spreadsheets and expensive monthly SaaS subscriptions.

## Core Value Proposition
**"Stop guessing your prices. Get mathematical certainty on every quote, eliminate unprofitable jobs, and instantly know your true bottom line."**
This tool empowers business owners to make confident pricing decisions, ensuring profitability on every service.

## Key Features

The Profit Per Job Calculator includes several interconnected sheets, each providing critical insights:

### Dashboard Summary
*   **Purpose:** Provides a high-level overview of key financial metrics and profitability indicators.
*   **Outputs:** Average Profit per Job, Total Monthly Profit (Estimated), Break-Even Number of Jobs.

### Job Costing
*   **Purpose:** Calculates the direct costs and profitability of an individual job.
*   **Features:**
    *   **Price vs. Labor Breakdown:** Exact cost of employee time, including estimated labor hours and hourly rates.
    *   **Chemical Cost Tracking:** Micro-costing for specific exterior cleaning chemicals (SH, surfactants, degreasers).
    *   **Callback Cost Impact:** Quantifies the devastating margin impact of returning to a job site, including estimated callback hours and material costs.

### Overhead & Break-Even
*   **Purpose:** Determines the number of jobs and revenue required to cover all fixed and variable overhead costs.
*   **Features:**
    *   **Break-Even Calculator:** Clearly shows how many jobs are needed to cover monthly overhead.

### Pricing Strategy
*   **Purpose:** Helps determine if current pricing meets desired margins and generates tiered pricing.
*   **Features:**
    *   **"Are You Undercharging?" Reality Check:** A visual indicator that flags low-margin jobs, ensuring you're not paying customers to clean their houses.
    *   **Good/Better/Best Pricing Generator:** Automatically calculates 3-tier pricing packages based on base costs, proven to increase average ticket size.

### Equipment ROI
*   **Purpose:** Calculates the return on investment for new equipment purchases.
*   **Features:**
    *   Tracks purchase price, estimated lifespan, maintenance costs, and revenue increase/cost savings per job to determine jobs and time to break even.

### Employee Compensation
*   **Purpose:** Calculates total labor costs including base pay, commissions, and bonuses.
*   **Features:**
    *   Allows owners to safely calculate performance pay or percentage-based pay without eating into the company's net profit margin.

### Drive Time & Setup Allocation
*   **Purpose:** Factors unbillable time (drive time, setup/teardown) into the job cost.
*   **Features:**
    *   Automatically accounts for the "unbillable" time that often erodes margins in residential routing.

### Automated Pricing Data
*   **Purpose:** Estimates labor and chemical costs based on service area size and productivity rates, providing a suggested base price.

## Tech Stack
Microsoft Excel (Version 2016 or newer recommended for full feature compatibility).

## Setup and Usage

### Setup
1.  Install dependencies: `pip install -r requirements.txt`
2.  Run the generator: `python generate_calculator.py`
3.  The output will be `profit_per_job_calculator.xlsx`.

**Note:** The file `profit_per_job_calculator.xlsx` is generated by `generate_calculator.py`. Do not edit the Excel file directly if you want to preserve changes in version control; instead, update the Python script and regenerate it.

### Usage
Open `profit_per_job_calculator.xlsx` in Microsoft Excel. Navigate through the various sheets (Dashboard Summary, Job Costing, Overhead & Break-Even, Pricing Strategy, etc.) to input your specific business data and instantly gain insights into your profitability, pricing, and operational efficiency. Each sheet is designed with intuitive inputs and clear outputs to guide you.
